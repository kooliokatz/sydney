Local Food

Sydney serves up some of the most delicious, fresh and multi-cultural foods that Australia has to offer. However, there are many food stops that are known only to the locals and have to be put on your "must EAT" list!

Haven Cafe

Surry Hills (Central Station)

Source: http://www.ifatfoodchronicles.com.au

Everyone enjoys their coffee differently and Haven is dedicated to serving perfect coffees by modifying the aroma, acidity, sweetness, body & aftertaste to suit each personal need. Their Cold-Brew coffee is hitting Sydney by a storm and is perfect for those hot days. Other menu items like their beautiful tea concoctions and their Hong Kong style egg waffles are also a 'Must Try'. This Surrey Hills Cafe is opposite the Central Station and is often overlooked by passer-bys, so be sure to stop by for a drink or a snack if you ever have some spare time before your train.

Mary's

Newtown
Source: Rara954 | www.tripadvisor.com
Source: localpush.com.au

Mary's is an interesting American style burger bar. You will barely find any advertisement for Mary's anywhere and even on the building of the restaurant, there is no signage (if anything it actually says "This is NOT Mary's"). Yet, somehow, every Friday and Saturday night, there is a line leading out the door waiting for a seat. Mary's is the type of place that is so good that it is spread purely by word of mouth. Finding it can be hard if you are not a local (and finding a local who will indulge the secret spot is rare) but if you are determined to finding an amazing burger and don't mind a little adventure, the hint is exactly in the name.

There are 4 types of Mary's Burgers:
  • Mary's Burger
  • Chicken Burger
  • Cheese Burger
  • Vege Burger

Tenkomori Ramen House

Regent Place
Source: regentplace.com
Source: Peach-Water | http://www.peach-water.com

This small, cute and express corner ramen shop in Regent Place offers one of the best and most authentic styles of express ramen. The flavours are amazingly consistent considering the speed and price of the meals, plus the noodles are soft and chewy! Oh but don't forget the addictive broth! I personally suggest the tradition tonkotsu broth!

Chinese Night Market

Chinatown (Haymarket)
Source: Alana Dimou | www.broadsheet.com.au
Source: www.sydney.com

This weekly night market is rapidly becoming one of Sydney's major tourist attractions and there is definitely no doubt as to why. They have been offering people a little taste of Asia with their wide selection of cuisine ranging from Hong Kong to Hanoi, with yum cha, tepanyaki, takoyaki, sushi, Sichuan, pho and more.

For more information check their website HERE!

Taste of Malaya

Castle Hill

Source: Little Owlie | http://thelittleowlie.blogspot.com.au

A small restaurant in the very large Castle Towers that offers a mixture of different Asian cuisines from Malaysian to Chinese to Indian. After a large chain Thai restaurant was built right next to it, this once popular restaurant was now being majorly overshadowed. I have been coming to this place since I was a small child and has become a family tradition for me (I almost always order the same dish). From experience, I believe it is often the smaller and quieter places that take the time to offer you the best food possible.

Pie Tin

Newtown


The savoury pie selection changes every day as do the sweet pies. The sweet pies range from healthier options like the fruit pies to less healthy but still delicious chocolate ganache pies to even Malteser or Tim Tam souffle pies. They also hold a range of salads, side dishes and local brewed beers and wines.

Fish Market

Sydney
Source: https://petersfishmarket.com.au
Source: http://www.weekendnotes.com

Serving some of the freshest seafood produce in all of Australia, the Sydney Fish Market offers a range of stores selling fresh seafood, cooked seafood, sushi, bread, wine, deli and even yum cha. It's the largest market of its kind in the Southern Hemisphere and in the top 5 seafood market in terms of variety in the world.
